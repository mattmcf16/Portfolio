About Me

Hi there! My name is Matthew McFarland, a soon-to-be Computer Science graduate from South Jersey, poised to graduate a year early. Born into a family with a keen interest in technology, I found my passion for software development early on. The thrill of problem-solving and the joy of creating something new has always been the driving force behind my love for this field.

I'm particularly proud of a project where I conducted statistical analysis on S&P 500 stock data. This project allowed me to interpret real-world data, identify trends, and make predictions, showcasing my analytical skills and interest in data-driven decision making.

Machine Learning and AI are areas that fascinate me the most, and they were the initial sparks that ignited my interest in Computer Science. I'm currently working on a neural network designed to predict market data, which perfectly combines my passion for technology and economy.

Outside the realm of coding, I'm an active individual who loves sports, gym workouts, and training in Brazilian Jiu-Jitsu. I also enjoy video games and have a strong interest in game design. As a futurist, I am always keen on learning about emerging technologies and exploring how they can shape our future.

Living with ADHD has been both a challenge and a blessing. It has at times presented hurdles, particularly when it comes to organization. However, I've learned to leverage it as one of my greatest strengths. It fuels my desire to keep my brain stimulated, a trait that constantly drives me to work harder and push my boundaries. My high school wrestling years played a pivotal role in shaping this aspect of my personality, teaching me that perseverance, discipline, and a strong work ethic can overcome any challenge.

With these experiences, I've come to see myself as a hard-working individual committed to personal growth and continuous learning. As I stand on the threshold of the corporate world, I am eager to bring my skills, passion, and dedication to an environment where I can make a tangible difference. Despite acknowledging my areas for improvement, I see them not as weaknesses, but as opportunities for growth. With a future that promises myriad learning opportunities, I am excited for the journey that lies ahead.
